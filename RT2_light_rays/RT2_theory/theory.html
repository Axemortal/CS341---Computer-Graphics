Theory Exercise Homework 2 (RT2)

Lighting and Light Rays

Derivation of the Iterative Formula

We are given that the resulting colour for a given pixel \(c_b\) is expressed as:

\[ \begin{equation} c_b = (1-\alpha_0)c_0 + \alpha_0 c^1 \end{equation} \]

Additionally, the value of \(c^1\) is defined by the equation:

\[ \begin{equation} c^1 = (1-\alpha_1)c_1 + \alpha_1 c^2 \end{equation} \]

Expanding this recursively, we get

\[ \begin{aligned} c_b &= (1-\alpha_0)c_0 + \alpha_0[(1-\alpha_1)c_1 + \alpha_1 c^2] \\ &= (1-\alpha_0)c_0 + \alpha_0(1-\alpha_1)c_1 + \alpha_0\alpha_1(1-\alpha_2)c_2 + \cdots \\ &= \sum_{i=0}^{+\infty} (1 - \alpha_i) \left(\prod_{k=0}^{i-1} \alpha_k \right) c_i \end{aligned} \]

Simplification for \(N\) Reflections

For \(N\) reflections, the resulting colour \(c_b\) is given by the following summation:

\[ \begin{equation} c_b = \sum_{i=0}^{N} (1 - \alpha_i) \left(\prod_{k=0}^{i-1} \alpha_k \right) c_i \end{equation} \]
